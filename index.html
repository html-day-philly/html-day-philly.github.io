When & Where

What is this?

This is the Philly venue for HTML Day, a worldwide HTML freewrite, and celebration.

Come to write HTML, learn HTML, talk about HTML, think about HTML, etc

To bring:

  • Water
  • Snacks
  • Writing supplies
  • Art supplies
  • A computer with a full battery
  • A friend :-)

Venue & Event Details:

Public Transport:
47 northbound and southbound stops half a block away, ~8 minute walk from 64 and 40 bus stops.
Parking:
Parallel street parking available, and it shouldn't be too too hard to find a spot around that time.
Entrances:
Entrance is on Catharine, closer to 8th. Sidewalk is a little bumpy getting there. The park is elevated, and can be accessed by 2 short steps, or a ramp on the eastern corner which has handrails on both sides.
Floor surface:
Brick and stone pavers, slightly bumpy but relatively even. No grass!
Bathrooms:
The Fleisher Art Memorial's entrance is just a few feet away, and has two accessible gender-neutral restrooms available to the public. Restrooms are accessible by a small set of stairs or a small lift that requires knocking for a key from the desk.
Seating:
The park has two metal picnic tables I'll try to reserve beforehand, one with two wheelchair-accessible seats. There are also many benches! All seating is slatted, including tabletops.
Lighting:
Lots of trees and plants, park is quite shady in the afternoon.
Participation:
Informal, come as you are, no centralized activity just talk about whatever you want with whoever you want! I (Yone) only speak english and HTML, and cannot personally bring any kind of interpreter.
Food:
Bring whatever snacks you'd like (unless it's raining and we're in the library). Please be aware and communicative of allergens!! Definitely bring water—there is also a water bottle refilling station in Fleisher. There is also a lot of tasty food in the vicinity, including John's Water Ice around the corner which is a nice treat for anybody.
Childcare:
Kids are so welcome! Personally, I teach first grade and love talking to kids of all ages about websites! This park doesn't have a playground of any kind, and might be kind of bland for kids unless they like looking at plants and interacting with very bold squirrels & birds. Expect to see lots of people eating water ice.
General assistance:
Let me know if you need any help of any kind, I am happy to provide direction and assistance getting into the park, but cannot transport anyone there (contact info in next section, email me ahead of time if you want my phone number!)
Wifi:
No wifi but... maybe. Don't depend on it!
Electricity:
No outlets! Bring a charged laptop :-).
