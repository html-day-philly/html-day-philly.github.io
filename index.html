What is this?

This is the Philly venue for HTML Day, a worldwide HTML freewrite, and celebration.

Come to write HTML, learn HTML, talk about HTML, think about HTML, etc

To bring:

  • Water
  • Snacks
  • Writing supplies
  • Art supplies
  • A computer with a full battery
  • A friend :-)

About the park:

  • Public Transport: 47 northbound and southbound stops half a block away, ~8 minute walk from 64 and 40 bus stops.
  • Parking: Parallel street parking available, and maybe the Fleisher Art Memorial lot if you ask (this event is not officially affiliated with Fleisher).
  • Entrances: Entrance is on Catharine, closer to 8th. Sidewalk is a little bumpy getting there. The park is elevated, and can be accessed by 2 short steps, or a ramp on the eastern corner which has handrails on both sides.
  • Floor surface: brick and stone pavers, slightly bumpy but relatively even. No grass!
  • Bathrooms: Fleisher's entrance is just a few feet away, and has two accessible gender-neutral restrooms available to the public. Restrooms are accessible by a small set of stairs or a small lift that requires knocking for a key from the desk.
  • Seating: The park has two picnic tables I'll try to reserve beforehand, one with two wheelchair-accessible seats. There are also many benches!  
  • Shade: Lots of trees and plants, park is quite shady in the afternoon.
  • Wifi: I have the Fleisher wifi password (shhhhhh) and it reaches the full park, but I wouldn't depend on it.
  • Electricity: No outlets! Bring a charged laptop :-).

Charles Santore Library facilities and accessibility info can be found here.
